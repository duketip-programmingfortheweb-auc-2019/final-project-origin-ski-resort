Our Story

Our founder, Edison Connor was a professonal gold medal skiier in the early 1900's. He firmly believed in donating to charites and orphanages as he was an orphan himself. A couple months before he had decided to retire, he had a nearly fatal accident at a charity fundraiser. He had promised to give the fundraiser more money if he succeded in breaking the world record for longest ski jump. In order to break the record, he started to gather speed, but he didn't notice a sharp turn in the course, and accidentaly crashed into some trees. He was left paralyzed from the waist down, and did not break his record. With the money that he had gathered from his professional days, he decided to create a resort. He opened the Origin Ski Resort on the second anniversary of his crash, and promised to give all of his profits from the resort to the Colorado Orphanage Institute. Over the course of a couple decades, he went to rehab constantly, and finally he overcame his paralysis. Though he never was able to professionaly ski again, he did ski every so often in his resort, greeting his guests daily.
